Thomas Reynolds, English sailor and merchant, arrived in Portugal in 1820, attracted by the wine business and has two children, Thomas and Robert. It is from Porto that it supplies its commercial warehouse in London with Iberian products. In 1838, they introduced themselves to the cork industry in Spain, expanding to Portugal, eventually settling in Estremoz. After the family left for New Zealand, Robert stayed in Estremoz in charge of the business, which he quickly developed with the acquisition of new lands and the production of quality wines, an activity he carried out with particular commitment and passion. Alentejo becomes the definitive birthplace of the Reynolds of Portugal and Robert, the patriarch of the family. From this cradle come Robert's eldest son, Robert Rafael, and from this, Carlos. Carlos has a first daughter named Gloria, Gloria Reynolds. In honor of him, and of all his ancestors who lived in Alentejo, Julian, son of Gloria, produces a quality wine, named after his mother Gloria Reynolds.
