Maison Moët & Chandon, founded in 1743, is one of the largest champagne producers in the world. Annually produces two million cases of champagne and in 1962 was the first producer on the French Stock Exchange. The name Chandon was added to the company when Jean-Rémy Moët ceded half the company to his son-in-law Pierre-Gabriel Chandon de Briailles in 1832, and the other half to his son Victor Moët. In 1840 the brand introduced the concept of vintage champagne and in 1842 it launched the first. Its best-known label, Dom Perignon, is a world reference that pays homage to the famous Benedictine monk, the “father of Champagne”. Maison Moet & Chandon merged with Hennessy of Cognac in 1971 and Louis Vuitton in 1987 to become LVMH (Louis-Vuitton-Moet-Hennessy), the world's largest luxury goods group. Moet & Chandon is the official champagne supplier to Queen Elizabeth II.
